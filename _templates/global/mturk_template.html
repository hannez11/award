This HIT is an experimental study conducted by Justus-Liebig-University Giessen.
It contains three parts: 1) playing a lottery 2) working on tasks concerning decision-making 3) answering some concluding questions.

Please schedule around 40 minutes for completing this HIT.
The compensation amounts to $7.50 on average ($1.55 fix payment for completing the HIT + $5.95 variable payment on average based on your decisions during the HIT).

Requirements to succesfully complete the HIT:

- You should be familiar with the concept of Expected Values. In the very beginning of this HIT, you have to answer a qualifiying question. If you do not qualify, you won't be able to continue the experiment. In this case, please return the HIT, which does not have any negative consequences on your MTurk rating.

- You will be paid if you 1) complete the experiment in full with thoughtful reponses 2) enter a valid confirmation code in the box below.

- If it becomes apparent that you work inattentivly on this HIT (e.g. rushing through pages etc.), you will not be able to complete the HIT and thus won't receive any compensation. Several checks have been imbedded to test attentiveness.

- We do not want to reject any workers, but inattentivly working on this HIT is a violation of these requirements and will result in imminent rejection.

- So please make sure that you focus on this HIT and complete it without any interruptions.

- Mobile devices are not allowed.

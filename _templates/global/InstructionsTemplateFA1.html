Information about your task

In this experiment, you are taking on the role of a leading project manager at the company CleverClean Inc.

In order to generate further growth within the next years, the management board intends to launch a new household utensil. Since you are the manager of the projects department, the board instructs you to choose and manage one out of two potential innovation projects. At CleverClean Inc. projects are selected based on their expected return. Hence, projects with higher expected returns are preferred.

Further, as part of CleverClean's corporate strategy, the board especially puts emphasis on the following aspects:

  • Achievement of high customer and employee satisfaction at any time
  • Encouragement of employees to be innovative
  • Reduction of resource wastage in failing projects

As financing capacities are limited at CleverClean Inc., a project is considered as failing if investing any additional dollar in this project creates lower expected returns than investing in alternative projects.

You will receive information about the two project ideas after completing the quiz. Based on this information, you will have to decide which of the two projects CleverClean Inc. should invest in. Once you have decided on one of the two projects, it will be launched. After you selected one project, you have to make up to two follow-up decisions whether you want to continue your project.

Important

Please be aware that your decisions might be reviewed by the experimental administrator. In this case you will receive written feedback on your decisions after you finished the experiment.

Compensation and experimental currency

During the main task, project related data is reported in the experimental currency Lira. Correspondingly, your compensation is first determined in the experimental currency Lira and will be converted into Dollar at the end of the experiment. 10,000 Lira is equivalent to one Dollar.

Your total compensation consists of the following three components:

1. Lottery payment

You already participated in the lottery. The lottery outcome will be displayed at the end of the experiment.

2. Fixed payment

At the end of the experiment you will receive a fixed payment of 15,500 Lira.

3. Variable payment

The variable compensation is based on the project's outcome, which is affected by your decisions during the main task.

The project's outcome is calculated as follows:

  • Project outcome = Cash inflows - Required investment

At the end of the experiment you receive a variable compensation, which equals 1% of the project's outcome. Additionally, you have the chance to receive a reward which will be explained on the next page. Thus, the variable compensation is calculated as follows:

  • Variable compensation in Lira = (Project outcome + potential award) × 1%

At the end of the experiment you receive detailed information on your compensation.

Example for the variable compensation:
  • The project's expected future cash inflows amount to 7m Lira.
  • The required investment of a project is 2m Lira.
  • The project realizes cash inflows of 7m Lira. Thus, the project outcome is 5m Lira.
  • Additionally, you qualify to receive the award which amounts to 0.2m Lira.

Your variable compensation is 1% of the project outcome plus the award, which equals to 52,000 Lira. Accordingly, you would receive a variable compensation of $5.20 in this experiment.
